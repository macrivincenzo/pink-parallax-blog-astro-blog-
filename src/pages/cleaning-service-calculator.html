SDE Analysis for Cleaning Services

Seller's Discretionary Earnings (SDE) is the most accurate method for cleaning service valuation. Unlike traditional businesses, cleaning services have unique financial structures including recurring revenue models, variable labor costs, and owner-operator compensation that require specialized analysis.

Cleaning Service SDE Formula

SDE = Net Income + Owner's Salary + Benefits + Depreciation + Interest + Non-recurring Expenses + Owner's Personal Expenses

Key Cleaning Service SDE Adjustments

Adjustment Category Description Typical Range
Owner's Compensation Fair market salary for owner-operator $60K - $120K
Benefits & Perks Health insurance, retirement, vehicle allowance $15K - $35K

Understanding Cleaning Service Cash Flow

Cleaning services have unique cash flow characteristics that impact valuation:

  • Recurring Revenue: 60-85% of revenue from regular weekly/bi-weekly clients
  • Customer Payment Terms: Mix of weekly, monthly, and one-time payments
  • Labor Intensity: 50-70% of revenue goes to direct labor costs
  • Seasonal Variations: Higher demand in spring, back-to-school, and holiday seasons
  • Supply Costs: 3-8% of revenue for cleaning supplies and equipment

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average cleaning service valuation in 2025?

Cleaning service valuations typically range from $150K to $3M+, with most services valued between $300K and $1.2M. The exact value depends on service type, client mix, recurring revenue percentage, location, and financial performance. Residential services average 2.0x-3.5x SDE, while commercial services can command 2.2x-4.0x SDE multiples.

How do I calculate SDE for my cleaning service?

SDE (Seller's Discretionary Earnings) = Net Income + Owner's Salary + Benefits + Depreciation + Interest + Non-recurring Expenses + Personal Expenses. For cleaning services, be sure to add back owner's vehicle expenses, personal supplies, owner's health insurance, family members' compensation above market rate, and depreciation on equipment and vehicles.

How important is recurring revenue percentage for valuation?

Extremely important. Services with 75%+ recurring revenue (weekly, bi-weekly, monthly contracts) command 25-40% premium valuations. Services with under 50% recurring revenue face valuation discounts. Recurring revenue provides predictable cash flow, reduces marketing costs, and indicates strong client relationships - all critical value drivers.
