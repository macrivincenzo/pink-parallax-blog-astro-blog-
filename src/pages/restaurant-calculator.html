SDE Analysis for Restaurant Businesses

Seller's Discretionary Earnings (SDE) is the most accurate method for restaurant valuation. Unlike traditional businesses, restaurants have unique financial structures that require specialized analysis.

Restaurant SDE Formula

SDE = Net Income + Owner's Salary + Benefits + Depreciation + Interest + Non-recurring Expenses + Owner's Personal Expenses

Key Restaurant SDE Adjustments

Adjustment Category Description Typical Range
Owner's Compensation Fair market salary for restaurant owner $60K - $150K+
Benefits & Perks Health insurance, retirement, car allowance $15K - $50K
Personal Expenses Personal meals, entertainment, travel $10K - $40K
Non-recurring Items Renovations, legal fees, one-time costs Varies

Restaurant Business Valuation Methods

Professional restaurant appraisers use three primary valuation approaches:

1. Income Approach (Most Common)

  • Capitalization Method: SDE √∑ Capitalization Rate
  • Discounted Cash Flow: Projected future cash flows discounted to present value
  • Best for: Established restaurants with predictable income

2. Market Approach

  • Compare to similar restaurants in the market
  • Use industry multiples and benchmarks
  • Requires access to comparable transaction data

3. Asset Approach

  • Value of tangible and intangible assets
  • Includes equipment, furniture, leasehold improvements
  • Less common for going concerns

Step-by-Step Restaurant Valuation Process

Step 1: Financial Analysis

  1. Gather Financial Statements: 3-5 years of P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow
  2. Calculate SDE: Apply all necessary adjustments
  3. Analyze Trends: Identify growth patterns and seasonality
  4. Normalize Expenses: Remove non-recurring and personal items

Common Restaurant Valuation Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Financial Analysis Mistakes

  • Using Revenue Multiples Only: Ignores profitability and operational efficiency
  • Incorrect SDE Adjustments: Missing owner compensation or personal expenses
  • Ignoring Seasonality: Not normalizing for seasonal variations
  • One-Time Event Impact: Including non-recurring revenue or expenses

‚ùå Restaurant Assessment Mistakes

  • Overvaluing Location: Prime location doesn't guarantee success
  • Undervaluing Brand: Established brand recognition adds significant value
  • Equipment Overvaluation: Restaurant equipment depreciates rapidly
  • Ignoring Lease Terms: Favorable vs. unfavorable lease impacts value

‚ùå Market Analysis Mistakes

  • Outdated Comparables: Using old market data
  • Wrong Geography: Comparing to dissimilar markets
  • Ignoring Competition: Not considering market saturation
  • Technology Blind Spots: Not considering delivery and digital trends

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average restaurant valuation in 2025?

Restaurant valuations typically range from $200K to $5M+, with most restaurants valued between $400K and $1.2M. The exact value depends on concept, location, financial performance, and market conditions.

How is restaurant valuation different from other businesses?

Restaurants have unique factors including food costs, labor intensity, seasonality, location dependency, and brand reputation. Valuations must account for these hospitality-specific elements.

What's the difference between SDE and EBITDA for restaurants?

SDE (Seller's Discretionary Earnings) includes owner compensation and personal expenses, making it more accurate for small restaurants. EBITDA excludes these items and is better for larger, institutional restaurants.

How often should I get my restaurant valued?

Professional valuations should be updated every 2-3 years or when significant changes occur (concept changes, major renovations, market shifts, or planned transactions).

What factors most impact restaurant value?

The top factors are: (1) Financial performance and SDE, (2) Location and foot traffic, (3) Concept and brand strength, (4) Operational efficiency and systems, (5) Market competition and conditions.

Can I use online calculators for restaurant valuation?

Online calculators provide estimates but lack the depth needed for actual transactions. Professional valuations are required for legal, tax, and financial purposes.

How long does a restaurant valuation take?

Professional valuations typically take 4-6 weeks, depending on restaurant complexity, data availability, and market research requirements.

What documents are needed for restaurant valuation?

Required documents include: 3-5 years of financial statements, tax returns, lease agreements, equipment lists, menu analysis, and operational metrics.
