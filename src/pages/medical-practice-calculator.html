SDE Analysis for Medical Practices

Seller's Discretionary Earnings (SDE) is the most accurate method for medical practice valuation. Unlike traditional businesses, medical practices have unique financial structures that require specialized analysis.

Medical Practice SDE Formula

SDE = Net Income + Owner's Salary + Benefits + Depreciation + Interest + Non-recurring Expenses + Owner's Personal Expenses

Key Medical Practice SDE Adjustments

Adjustment Category Description Typical Range
Owner's Compensation Fair market salary for physician owner $250K - $500K+
Benefits & Perks Health insurance, retirement, car allowance $50K - $150K
Personal Expenses Personal items run through business $20K - $100K
Non-recurring Items One-time expenses, legal fees, renovations Varies

Medical Practice Valuation Methods

Professional medical practice appraisers use three primary valuation approaches:

1. Income Approach (Most Common)

  • Capitalization Method: SDE ÷ Capitalization Rate
  • Discounted Cash Flow: Projected future cash flows discounted to present value
  • Best for: Established practices with predictable income

2. Market Approach

  • Compare to similar practices in the market
  • Use industry multiples and benchmarks
  • Requires access to comparable transaction data

3. Asset Approach

  • Value of tangible and intangible assets
  • Includes equipment, real estate, patient records
  • Less common for going concerns

Step-by-Step Medical Practice Valuation Process

Step 1: Financial Analysis

  1. Gather Financial Statements: 3-5 years of P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow
  2. Calculate SDE: Apply all necessary adjustments
  3. Analyze Trends: Identify growth patterns and seasonality
  4. Normalize Expenses: Remove non-recurring and personal items

Step 2: Practice Assessment

  1. Patient Base Analysis: Demographics, insurance mix, retention rates
  2. Service Mix Review: High-margin vs. low-margin services
  3. Staff Evaluation: Key personnel, retention, productivity
  4. Equipment Assessment: Age, condition, replacement needs

Step 3: Market Analysis

  1. Competitive Landscape: Local market conditions and competition
  2. Demographic Trends: Population growth, aging, insurance changes
  3. Regulatory Environment: Reimbursement changes, compliance requirements
  4. Technology Trends: Telehealth, AI, automation adoption

Step 4: Valuation Calculation

Practice Value = SDE × Specialty Multiple × Location Factor × Quality Factor

Common Medical Practice Valuation Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Financial Analysis Mistakes

  • Using Revenue Multiples Only: Ignores profitability and operational efficiency
  • Incorrect SDE Adjustments: Missing owner compensation or personal expenses
  • Ignoring Seasonality: Not normalizing for seasonal variations
  • One-Time Event Impact: Including non-recurring revenue or expenses

❌ Practice Assessment Mistakes

  • Overvaluing Patient Lists: Patient records have limited standalone value
  • Undervaluing Staff: Key personnel significantly impact practice value
  • Equipment Overvaluation: Medical equipment depreciates rapidly
  • Ignoring Lease Terms: Favorable vs. unfavorable lease impacts value

❌ Market Analysis Mistakes

  • Outdated Comparables: Using old market data
  • Wrong Geography: Comparing to dissimilar markets
  • Ignoring Regulatory Changes: Reimbursement changes affect value
  • Technology Blind Spots: Not considering digital transformation

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average medical practice valuation in 2025?

Medical practice valuations typically range from $400K to $2.5M+, with most practices valued between $800K and $1.5M. The exact value depends on specialty, location, patient base, and financial performance.

How is medical practice valuation different from other businesses?

Medical practices have unique factors including regulatory compliance, patient relationships, insurance reimbursement, and professional goodwill. Valuations must account for these healthcare-specific elements.

What's the difference between SDE and EBITDA for medical practices?

SDE (Seller's Discretionary Earnings) includes owner compensation and personal expenses, making it more accurate for small medical practices. EBITDA excludes these items and is better for larger, institutional practices.

How often should I get my medical practice valued?

Professional valuations should be updated every 2-3 years or when significant changes occur (new partners, major equipment purchases, market changes, or planned transactions).

What factors most impact medical practice value?

The top factors are: (1) Financial performance and SDE, (2) Patient base quality and retention, (3) Medical specialty and procedure mix, (4) Location and market conditions, (5) Staff quality and operational efficiency.

Can I use online calculators for medical practice valuation?

Online calculators provide estimates but lack the depth needed for actual transactions. Professional valuations are required for legal, tax, and financial purposes.

How long does a medical practice valuation take?

Professional valuations typically take 4-6 weeks, depending on practice complexity, data availability, and market research requirements.

What documents are needed for medical practice valuation?

Required documents include: 3-5 years of financial statements, tax returns, patient demographics, staff information, equipment lists, lease agreements, and operational metrics.
